# Abstract Draft V1

We study lightweight title-aware lexical reranking for official FEVER evidence retrieval. Starting from a BM25 candidate retriever, we ask whether simple title-aware lexical signals can improve strict gold-page ranking quality without changing candidate-set coverage. Our main independent evaluation uses a disjoint 1000-query official FEVER validation split and a strict metric that requires exact alignment with official evidence pages after title normalization. Under this setting, a token-level title-overlap reranker improves strict Recall@1 from 0.368 to 0.415 and strict Recall@5 from 0.627 to 0.680 over BM25, while strict Recall@10 remains unchanged at 0.720, indicating that the gain comes from better early-rank ordering rather than broader retrieval coverage. The same comparison is statistically supported by paired testing and bootstrap confidence intervals, and the gains appear for both SUPPORTS and REFUTES. We also evaluate a nearest simpler title-aware lexical baseline based on exact title boosting and find that it is stronger than token-level title overlap on the same split. The resulting conclusion is therefore narrow but stable: lightweight title-aware lexical reranking is effective for official FEVER evidence retrieval, while title overlap should be understood as a conservative variant rather than the uniquely best tested method.
