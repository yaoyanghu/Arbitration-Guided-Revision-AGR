## Summary

The dominant formal errors are:

1. wrong entity or wrong evidence family under near-duplicate statistic passages
2. wrong value extracted from the correct evidence family
3. stale secondary evidence leaking into final answer wording
4. temporal relation or year-selection mistakes on multi-year evidence

These patterns indicate that the current bottleneck is no longer pipeline connectivity. The remaining weaknesses are concentrated in answer discrimination and relation-sensitive temporal choice.
